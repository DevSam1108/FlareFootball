  <name>Task 2: Verify trail behavior on iPhone 12</name>

  <action>
CHECKPOINT: Human verification required. Deploy the app to iPhone 12 via Xcode and run through the test protocol below.

What was built: Full trail visualization on the YOLO detection screen — fading dot trail with connecting lines, occlusion gap handling, class priority filtering, and auto-clear after extended ball loss.

How to verify: Deploy the app to iPhone 12 via Xcode (Product > Run or install the .app from the build output). Open the app and navigate to the live detection screen. Hold the phone in landscape orientation.

**Test 1 — Trail appears (RNDR-01, RNDR-02, RNDR-05):**
Move a soccer ball slowly across the camera view. You should see:
- Orange dots appearing along the ball's path
- Recent dots are bright/opaque, older dots fade out
- Orange lines connecting the dots
- The trail follows the ball position accurately (dots centered on ball, not offset)
- PASS if: visible fading dot trail with connecting lines tracks the ball in landscape
- FAIL if: no trail visible, dots offset from ball, or lines missing

**Test 2 — Occlusion gap (TRAK-02, RNDR-03):**
Move the ball into view, then hide it behind your hand or move it out of frame for 1-2 seconds, then bring it back. You should see:
- Trail pauses when ball disappears (no new dots added during occlusion)
- When ball reappears, trail resumes with a visible gap (no connecting line across the gap)
- PASS if: trail pauses and resumes with a visible gap
- FAIL if: line drawn across the gap, or trail does not resume

**Test 3 — Auto-clear (TRAK-05):**
Move the ball completely out of frame and wait 3-5 seconds (30+ frames at detection rate). You should see:
- Trail fades away naturally as dots age out
- After ~1.5s the old dots have expired
- After ~30 consecutive missed frames, the trail clears completely
- PASS if: trail clears after several seconds with no ball
- FAIL if: ghost trail persists indefinitely

**Test 4 — Class priority (TRAK-03) [if tennis ball available]:**
If a tennis ball is available, place both a soccer ball and tennis ball in frame. The trail should follow the soccer ball, not the tennis ball.
- PASS if: trail tracks soccer ball
- FAIL if: trail tracks tennis ball
- SKIP if: no tennis ball available for testing

**Test 5 — Trail length and visual quality:**
Move the ball at moderate speed. The trail should show approximately 1-1.5 seconds of history. Dots should taper in size (newest ~7px, oldest ~2px).
- PASS if: trail length looks reasonable (~1.5s), dots taper, overall visual is clean
- FAIL if: trail is too short/long, visual artifacts, or jitter makes trail unusable

Resume signal: Report pass/fail for each test. Type "approved" if all critical tests pass (Tests 1-3). If issues found, describe what you see.
